Ramble Of The Week


Updates!



Been a while, hasn't it? Hell I know no one's gonna read this, at least in a little while, but if in future I got famous and people read my unrestrained stream of thoughts that is also known as this blog, hi! How you doing? Anyways, if you are reading this and have read the other posts, you might have wondered why there is a 2 week gap in posts, the answer for that is quite simple: I was too lazy to fork the site and keep updating it as my teacher graded the original github repo and code. Now that all that is done, I've learned my lesson, fork your stuff, but at least you get to laugh at my lazyness, which I imagine will likely not change much at all in the future.


But, alas, that is that and this is this. So what do I bring to you this week to justify my ramblings? Updates! I've got a few ideas and plans on what to do with the site and what to improve so I might as well tell you what I'm planning since I don't have anything else I want to put on the homepage right now and I don't feel like thinking up another deranged ramble about two topics that barely link together. Don't worry I'm sure I'll think of one in the meantime between now and next week's RoTW. Might make a post about music, we'll see.


Changes that you, the reader, will notice: Visual updates! Plenty of them coming (I think). I like design I've got going on right now, it's very functional and uncluttered, BUT it isn't perfect or all too close to finished or the final goal. Better navigation, a search bar, maybe some small sections on the left or right of the pages to quickly switch between posts, better methods of plugging my stuff (be it books or whatever else) in the future for when I finally get off my ass and publish something, maybe even a tagging system for a better search functionality, if I feel fancy some week, and also a new logo, I think I've got a really good pic of Caiman to put up there. Functionality! Actual and working forms, none of this stuff that I set up there in the gone Poll section only for my teacher to grade. I want to allow people to leave comments and send reports or mail of some kind through the site, though of course I have an email set up and should probably put it somewhere on the page, probably in the About section.

Other things I want to do are making the nav bar and footer be sticky to the top and bottom of the viewport or whatever you call it. Maybe only the header, not so sure about making the footer sticky. Changing the style of the header and maybe new fonts are two things that are also on the table. Not very happy with how barrent the header is right now, but that may change once I get a new logo. Better functionality on vertical displays! As of right now, the site is mostly perfectly functional on vertical displays, but at times functional isn't enough. Among some of the issues are the fact that the main text gets very squished between the two side bars of nothing, bars which work well on horizontal displays and add a little character (IMO) but on vertical displays take up valuable space for not much return.


Changes that I and anyone who decides to press F12 will notice: Better code! As far as I know my code is alright and that validator doesn't see any issue but that doesn't mean it can't get better. I'm sure there's some poor practices in it, probablt in the .css file, speaking of which, I hope to learn it better and be able to do some more stuff while knowing what I'm doing instead of taking it from stack overflow and taking it for granted. A better way to post! Writing code is fun but man I'm very sure I'll I'll be more inclined to throw stuff here if I have a markdown text editor or something in-site (for me and myself only of course). Or maybe not having one is better, means I'm forced to think more about what I want I'm going to post and I have more time to think about it as I write it out on code, ensure it's working right and upload it to github (or whatever hosting service I use in the future). Regardless, I'll have to get one working for the comment system. Another thing I want to do is make it so whenever I add a post it automatically gets linked in Rambles with an automatic preview of the first paragraph or so. Don't really know to what extent that is possible, but any little convinient QoL feature is appreciated.


Changes already done: New URL and repo! I've moved onto a new github repo that will make it easier to type out the URL, that, and minor text adjustments (left-align text and padding at the bottom of the page) are the two things I can think of out of the top of my head.


That's about it for now, I'm pretty sure. I'll put up some more interesting posts in Rambles in the near future. Until then, thanks for reading, and I'll see you next time!
